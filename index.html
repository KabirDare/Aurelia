Our Story

Two coastlines,
one kitchen.

Aurelia opened in 2015 with a simple provocation: what happens when the smoke and spice of the Konkan coast is disciplined by the sauces and structure of French haute cuisine? A decade later, the answer sits on a quiet street in Bandra, glass walls open to the Arabian Sea.

Every plate begins at the docks and markets of Sassoon and Crawford, then travels through a kitchen trained in Lyon and Mumbai alike. The result is neither fusion nor imitation — it is its own coastline.

Executive Chef

Vivaan Rao

Trained under a Bocuse d'Or finalist in Lyon before returning to the city he grew up in, Chef Rao spent two years fishing alongside Sassoon Dock traders to understand the coast he wanted to cook. Aurelia is the result — a kitchen that treats Konkan ingredients with the same rigor as a Lyonnaise sauce station.

"I don't want French food with Indian spices sprinkled on top. I want a dish where you can't tell which coastline taught it discipline, and which one gave it heat."
